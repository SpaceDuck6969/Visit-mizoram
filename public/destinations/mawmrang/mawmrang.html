Hriangmual Peak (Mawmrang Tlang)

Where Forests, Stones, and Stories Endure

Hidden in the eastern reaches of Mizoram, far from crowded trails and familiar itineraries, rises a quiet giant known as Hriangmual Peak. Standing on the outskirts of Phuaibuang village in Saitual District, this majestic mountain remains largely untouched—guarded by forests, folklore, and time itself.

Located about 180 km from Aizawl, Hriangmual is a destination for those who seek silence, mystery, and landscapes still ruled by nature.

A Name Rooted in the Forest

The name Hriangmual is drawn from its surroundings—“Hriang”, an evergreen tree that flourishes here, and “Mual”, meaning ground. Together, they reflect a land shaped by trees, earth, and enduring life. The peak stands tall just beyond Phuaibuang village, its slopes blanketed with dense evergreen forest that remains green through every season.

A Living Wilderness

Hriangmual is rich in both flora and fauna. The forest is home to stag, deer, wild boar, monkeys, and numerous bird species, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Flowing quietly through the forest is the Talan River, adding to the area’s untouched beauty and ecological importance.

One of the most intriguing features of the forest is Fangfar Puk, a natural cave approximately 15–20 feet wide. Inside, stalagmites rise from the cave floor, with water slowly dripping from them. Locals collect this water, believing it possesses healing properties, and it is traditionally used in the treatment of various illnesses—a practice passed down through generations.

Stones That Speak of the Past

Hriangmual is not only a natural sanctuary, but also a historical one. It is believed that the Thado (Ngendal) tribe once lived in these forests during the late 1800s. Scattered across the forest are thousands of monolithic remains, silent witnesses to a forgotten chapter of history.

Among these are six preserved stone tables, still visible today. Nearby stand larger monoliths encircled by countless smaller stones, suggesting ritual or communal significance. The INTACH Mizoram Chapter is currently conducting detailed studies of these structures, hoping to shed new light on early Mizo history and cultural roots.

Recognising its importance, Phuaibuang village authorities strictly monitor and protect the area, ensuring that Hriangmual’s natural and historical heritage remains intact for future generations.

The Flat Summit of Mawmrang Tlang

Also known as Mawmrang Tlang, the mountain rises to 1,812 meters, making it the 11th highest peak in Mizoram. What sets it apart is its surprisingly flat summit, a rare feature that gives visitors an expansive sense of space and calm.

The peak can be reached either by hiking or by following a nature trail, rewarding travelers with sweeping views and profound stillness. From the top, the hills seem endless, and the silence feels almost sacred.

Why Visit Hrangmual(Mawmrang Tlang)

Hriangmual is not a place of noise or spectacle.It is a place of presence.

Here, forests guard ancient stones, caves drip healing water, and flat mountaintops invite reflection. It is a reminder that Mizoram still holds landscapes beyond imagination—waiting quietly to be discovered.

For those willing to go beyond the familiar, Hriangmual Peak stands ready, timeless and watchful, surpassing every expectation.

Travel Guide to Mawmrang Tlang

Best Time to Visit

October – March

  • Offers the most pleasant experience, with cool weather, clearer skies, and easier trekking conditions. The forests remain lush, and visibility from the summit is at its best.

Ideal For:

  • Eco-tourism
  • Trekking
  • Wildlife observation
  • Heritage exploration,
  • Photography
  • Solitude

Best Time of Day

Early morning is ideal for starting your journey, allowing you to enjoy the calm forest atmosphere and reach the summit before midday. Late afternoon is also rewarding, as the soft light enhances the beauty of the landscape and creates a peaceful, reflective mood.

How to Reach Mawmrang Tlang

By Air: The nearest airport is Lengpui Airport in Aizawl, approximately 66 km away. Taxis are available to reach the village.

By Road: Zawlpala Thlantlang is about 37 km from Aizawl. A scenic drive through the hills takes an hour.

By Train: The nearest major railway station is in Sairang, around 56-65 km away. From there, travel by road.

What to Carry

  • Carry sufficient water, basic supplies, and comfortable trekking gear, as facilities near the peak are limited. It is also advisable to coordinate with local guides or village authorities for a safe and respectful visit.
