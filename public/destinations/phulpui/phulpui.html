Phulpui Village

Where Nature and Culture Live Together

Phulpui is one of Mizoram’s most touching and meaningful attractions, deeply connected with local legends and timeless love stories. Situated in Aizawl district, this unique site is known for its two historic graves that symbolize devotion and eternal love.

Often compared to India’s great monuments of romance, Phulpui holds a special place in the hearts of visitors, especially couples. Nestled amidst scenic natural surroundings, the site offers both emotional significance and peaceful beauty.

Easily accessible from Aizawl, Phulpui is considered one of the must-visit destinations in Mizoram. Its blend of history, folklore, and serene landscapes makes it a memorable stop for travelers exploring the region.

Legend And Stories

Long ago, in the hills of Mizoram, lived Zawlpala, a young man deeply in love with Tualvungi, a maiden celebrated for her beauty and grace. The two were married and lived happily, bound by affection and devotion. Their peace, however, was shattered when Phuntiha, a powerful and feared ruler from Tripura, heard of Tualvungi’s beauty and travelled to their village with the intention of claiming her.

Fearing for his life, Zawlpala falsely introduced Tualvungi as his sister. Phuntiha immediately demanded her hand in marriage and asked for the traditional bride price. Hoping to drive the tyrant away, Zawlpala named an impossibly large bride price—mithans, ornaments, woven puans, and weapons in overwhelming numbers. To his horror, Phuntiha accepted the demand and soon returned with a grand entourage, fulfilling every condition.

Despite her pleas, Tualvungi was taken away and forced into marriage with Phuntiha. Zawlpala, bound by his own words, watched helplessly as his beloved was led away.Although Phuntiha treated Tualvungi generously, she never ceased to mourn her first love. Consumed by jealousy, Phuntiha eventually invited Zawlpala to his village under the guise of hospitality. During the visit, Zawlpala was secretly poisoned and later died, revealing the truth before his death. He was buried with honour, accompanied by the traditional thlaichhiah ritual, where animals were sacrificed so their spirits might guide him into the next world.

News of Zawlpala’s death was carried to Tualvungi by a wood pigeon, a bird believed in folklore to be the chosen messenger of sorrow. Heartbroken, she resolved to return to her former village. Though Phuntiha delayed her repeatedly and even injured her to prevent her escape, Tualvungi endured every hardship and eventually set out alone on a long and painful journey.

Guided by villagers and children along the way, she finally reached Zawlpala’s grave-marked by blooming trees and rows of mithan skulls. Overcome by grief, she asked an elderly woman to end her life so she could be buried beside her beloved. Her wish was granted, and the two were united in death.

Legend says that the spirits of Zawlpala and Tualvungi transformed into butterflies, forever free and together. Phuntiha, unable to let go even in death, followed them—giving rise to the belief that when two butterflies fly together, a third often trails behind.

Why Visit Zawlpala Thlan Tlang

Beyond its powerful legend, Phulpui Tlang offers visitors a peaceful hilltop escape overlooking rolling green landscapes of Mizoram. The site is surrounded by gentle slopes, scattered trees, and open skies that create a calm and reflective atmosphere.

Visitors can enjoy:

  • Panoramic views of nearby hills and valleys
  • Cool hilltop breeze and quiet natural surroundings
  • Perfect sunrise and golden hour photography moments
  • A tranquil setting ideal for couples and heritage lovers
  • A short scenic drive from Aizawl with pleasant countryside views

The hilltop is especially beautiful in the early morning and late afternoon, when mist drapes the hills and the sun paints the landscape in warm tones. During these moments, the story of Zawlpala and Tualvungi feels almost alive in the silence of nature.

Phulpui Tlang is not just a historical site—it is an emotional and scenic destination where folklore meets landscape. Whether you come for love, culture, photography, or quiet reflection, the hill welcomes you with both beauty and memory.

Travel Guide to Zawlpala Thlantlang

Best Time to Visit

October – March (Ideal Season)

  • Pleasant weather (cool and comfortable)
  • Clear skies with beautiful hill views
  • Perfect for photography and sightseeing
  • Comfortable for walking and exploring

Winter mornings often bring light mist across the hills, creating a magical atmosphere. Sunsets during this period are especially scenic and peaceful.

June – September (Monsoon Season)

  • Lush greenery and vibrant landscapes
  • Frequent rainfall
  • Slippery roads and limited visibility at times

While the hills look fresh and green during monsoon, travel may be slightly less convenient due to rain.

April – May (Pre-Monsoon)

  • Warmer temperatures
  • Clear views in early mornings and evenings
  • Less crowded

Best Time of Day

Early morning and late afternoon are the most beautiful times to visit. Sunrise brings soft mist over the hills, while sunset paints the landscape in warm golden hues.

How to Reach Zawlpala Thlantlang

By Air: The nearest airport is Lengpui Airport in Aizawl, approximately 66 km away. Taxis are available to reach the village.

By Road: Zawlpala Thlantlang is about 37 km from Aizawl. A scenic drive through the hills takes an hour.

By Train: The nearest major railway station is in Sairang, around 56-65 km away. From there, travel by road.

What to Carry

  • Comfortable trekking shoes
  • Light jackets, especially in winter
  • Raincoat during monsoon
  • Camera for capturing landscapes
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Basic first-aid kit
