Stay informed

Travel calm, climb smart, know who to call.

Mizoram is widely regarded as one of India’s safer states for visitors. Still, hills, season, and remoteness matter. Use this page as a practical checklist—not a substitute for official advisories, your embassy, or local authorities.

emergency Emergency numbers

All‑India & state

  • Emergency (single number in many states): 112
  • Ambulance (often public): 108
  • Fire: 101

For police in a specific town, locals and your hotel can give the nearest thana (station) number—save it on arrival.

local_hospital Medical care

In Aizawl

Larger facilities include Civil Hospital Aizawl and private options such as Ebenezer Medical Centre and Trinity Hospital (names and services change—verify before travel). For serious emergencies, stabilize first, then follow local advice on transfer.

Remote areas

Carry a basic kit, prescribed medicines, and ORS. Mobile coverage can be patchy on ridges—download offline maps and tell someone your route and expected return time.

landscape Hills & weather

Trekking & viewpoints

  • Check fog, rain, and sunset time; carry a torch and rain layer.
  • Stick to known paths; hire local guides for longer or restricted routes.
  • Altitude and steep descents sap energy—plan shorter days if you are new to hills.

diversity_3 Respect & social calm

Community norms

Sundays are quiet; dress modestly at places of worship; ask before photographing people or private events. Courtesy and patience go a long way on narrow mountain roads.

badge Permits & documents

Inner Line Permit (ILP)

Domestic visitors usually need an ILP for Mizoram. Rules change—confirm on official state portals before you book transport.
